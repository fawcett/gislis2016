License

In the US, you get copyright on creative works, but not on 'facts'.
The point at which a collection of facts becomes a creative work is contentious, so it is important to be explicit.

Open License vs Copyright

  • Copyright != Open
  • Federal data, by law, is not covered by copyright
  • Local data could be, important to waive explicitly

Open licenses

  • Public Domain
  • Optional: attribution
  • Optional: share-alike
From OpenDefinition.org.

Public Domain

  • No restrictions on use
  • (Explicitly waiving any rights)
  • CC0 (4.0) or ODC PDDL
    Creative Commons and Open Data Commons Public Domain Dedication and License

Attribution Clause

  • Users MUST give attribution to you
  • CC-by (4.0) or ODC-by
  • Sounds good in theory
    • Attribution stacking
    • Difficult for aggregators (API, combining/deriving data)

CC0 (+by) ... enables creativity and reuse, with attribution still maintained by the vast majority of people ... part of a social contract.

Dan Cohen - Digital Public Library of America

'Social or Ethical' attribution CC0 (+by)

Dan Cohen, Executive Director of Digital Public Library http://www.dancohen.org/2013/11/26/cc0-by/

"bad actors will do bad things, regardless of license"

also, he underestimated the number of good actors

I think that CC0 (+by) is the best of both worlds: the data in a free-flowing environment that enables creativity and reuse, with attribution still maintained by the vast majority of people who consider themselves part of a social contract.

Share-Alike Clause

  • Users MUST share any derivatives under same license (viral)
  • CC-by-sa or ODbL
  • Causes challenges for users (management and redistribution)
